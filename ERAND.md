# ERAND — Erard Harp Specifications

Source: https://harpcanada.com/harpmaking/erard.htm (Joseph Jourdain, Josephus Harp Shop, 2019 analysis of a 1901 Paris drawing).

## Historical Background

- **Original drawing**: Paris, 1901, established from photographs of an Erard harp.
- **Analysis author**: Joseph Jourdain — Josephus Harp Shop (2019).
- **Period stringing**: Gut strings (nylon did not exist); compound wire strings beginning at E2 (string #36).

## String Tension Assumptions

- Tensile-strength ratio: **70%** (typical of concert harps, which are known for tight strings).
- Reference string: **0.025" gut**.
- String **#6 (B6♭)** identified as the point of maximum tensile strength.

## String Tensions

All strings are tuned to the specifications above. Tension spans from **10.976 lb** at string 1 (G7) to **52.693 lb** at string 47 (C1).

## Design Methodology (Five-Step Process)

1. Precise measurement from enlarged drawing (200%+ enlargement recommended).
2. Theoretical stringband calculation for the treble section.
3. Scale-ratio establishment via comparison method.
4. Conversion of all measurements to full size using the identified ratio.
5. Practical string-configuration planning.

### Measurement Precision

A transparent ruler with millimetre or 1/60" graduations plus a magnifying glass achieves precision of about **0.2 mm (0.008")**.

## Folk-Harp Adaptation

A 36-string folk version covering **C2 to C7** can be produced by:

- Removing the first 4 and last 7 strings.
- Converting the instrument entirely to nylon strings.
- Applying a **5.612% reduction factor** for natural notes.
